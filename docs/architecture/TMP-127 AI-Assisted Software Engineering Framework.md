# Preamble

Artificial Intelligence is transforming the way software is designed, documented, implemented, reviewed, and maintained.

Within TechAndMe, AI is viewed as an engineering assistant rather than an engineering replacement.

Its purpose is to improve productivity, increase consistency, accelerate learning, and support engineering quality while ensuring that human engineers remain responsible for all final decisions.

This framework establishes the standards for responsibly integrating AI into the TechAndMe engineering methodology.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this framework is to define how AI shall be incorporated into software engineering activities while maintaining engineering quality, accountability, and professional standards.

It provides guidance for using AI consistently across all TechAndMe projects.

---

# 2. Scope

This framework applies to:

- Software architecture
- Documentation
- Source code generation
- Refactoring
- Testing
- Code review
- Repository management
- Engineering analysis
- Engineering education
- Knowledge management

---

# 3. AI Engineering Principles

AI-assisted engineering shall follow these principles.

## Human Responsibility

Engineers remain accountable for every engineering decision.

---

## Architecture Before AI

AI should operate within an approved architecture rather than defining it independently.

---

## Verify Everything

AI-generated outputs shall be reviewed before adoption.

---

## Documentation First

Important AI-assisted decisions should be documented.

---

## Continuous Learning

AI interactions should contribute to improving engineering practices and the Playbook.

---

# 4. AI Roles Within Engineering

AI may assist with:

- Brainstorming
- Architecture discussions
- Documentation drafting
- Code generation
- Code explanation
- Code review
- Test generation
- Refactoring suggestions
- Risk identification
- Knowledge summarisation

AI should augment engineering capability rather than replace engineering judgement.

---

# 5. Human Oversight

All AI-generated outputs require appropriate human review.

Human responsibilities include:

- Validating technical correctness
- Confirming architectural compliance
- Assessing maintainability
- Identifying security concerns
- Reviewing business alignment
- Approving final implementation

Responsibility for engineering outcomes always remains with the engineer.

---

# 6. AI Engineering Workflow

The recommended workflow is:

1. Define the engineering objective.
2. Gather relevant context.
3. Prepare structured prompts.
4. Generate AI-assisted output.
5. Review and validate.
6. Revise where necessary.
7. Approve for adoption.
8. Document significant decisions.

This workflow encourages responsible AI usage while preserving engineering quality.

---

# 7. Prompt Engineering Standards

Effective prompts should include:

- Clear objectives
- Relevant context
- Architectural constraints
- Expected outputs
- Applicable engineering standards
- Acceptance criteria

Well-structured prompts improve the quality and consistency of AI responses.

---

# 8. AI Output Validation

Before adoption, AI-generated outputs should be evaluated for:

- Technical accuracy
- Architectural consistency
- Security implications
- Documentation quality
- Maintainability
- Compliance with TechAndMe standards
- Business alignment

Validation is mandatory regardless of the confidence of the AI response.

---

# 9. AI Governance

AI-assisted engineering shall comply with the TechAndMe governance framework.

Governance includes:

- Human approval
- Documentation of significant decisions
- Engineering reviews
- Architecture reviews
- Quality assurance
- Knowledge preservation

AI should strengthen governance rather than bypass it.

---

# 10. Continuous Improvement

AI-assisted engineering practices should evolve through:

- Engineering reviews
- Lessons learned
- Prompt refinement
- AI capability assessments
- Updates to engineering standards
- Expansion of reusable prompt libraries

Continuous improvement ensures that AI remains an effective engineering partner.

# 13. Closing Reflection

Artificial Intelligence is most valuable when paired with disciplined engineering.

It accelerates learning, supports implementation, and improves productivity, but it cannot replace thoughtful architecture, sound judgement, or professional responsibility.

Within TechAndMe, AI is regarded as a collaborative engineering partner that helps engineers build better software while continuously becoming better engineers themselves.
