# Preamble

Every engineering project involves uncertainty.

Changes in requirements, technical complexity, resource constraints, security threats, and external dependencies all introduce risks that can affect project success.

The Engineering Risk Management Framework establishes a structured approach for identifying, assessing, managing, and monitoring engineering risks throughout the lifecycle of every TechAndMe project.

Effective risk management enables informed decision-making, protects engineering quality, and improves project resilience.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this framework is to establish a consistent risk management process for all TechAndMe engineering projects.

The framework aims to:

- Identify engineering risks early.
- Reduce project uncertainty.
- Improve decision-making.
- Protect engineering quality.
- Support successful project delivery.
- Encourage proactive rather than reactive management.

---

# 2. Scope

This framework applies to:

- Software development projects.
- AI-enabled systems.
- Infrastructure projects.
- Documentation initiatives.
- Open-source repositories.
- Internal engineering tools.

Risk management should be integrated into every phase of the Software Development Lifecycle.

---

# 3. Risk Management Philosophy

Risk cannot be eliminated.

It can, however, be understood, evaluated, and managed.

Within TechAndMe:

- Risks should be identified early.
- Significant risks should be documented.
- Decisions should consider both probability and impact.
- Mitigation should be planned before implementation whenever practical.
- Risk reviews should occur continuously.

Managing risk is a normal engineering responsibility.

# 5. Risk Management Process

Every project should follow a structured risk management process.

## Step 1 — Identify Risks

Identify potential risks as early as possible.

Sources include:

- Architecture reviews.
- Engineering checkpoints.
- Requirements analysis.
- Lessons learned.
- Team discussions.

---

## Step 2 — Assess Risks

Evaluate each risk based on:

- Likelihood.
- Impact.
- Urgency.
- Detectability.

The assessment should guide prioritisation.

---

## Step 3 — Plan Responses

Determine the most appropriate response.

Possible strategies include:

- Avoid.
- Mitigate.
- Transfer.
- Accept.

The selected response should be documented.

---

## Step 4 — Implement Mitigation

Execute the agreed mitigation actions.

Mitigation activities should be tracked alongside normal project work.

---

## Step 5 — Monitor Risks

Review identified risks throughout the project lifecycle.

New risks may emerge as the project evolves.

Risk management is a continuous process.

---

# 6. Risk Register

Projects should maintain a risk register for significant risks.

A typical risk register should include:

| Field | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Risk ID | Unique identifier |
| Description | Summary of the risk |
| Category | Technical, Project, Security, etc. |
| Likelihood | Low, Medium, High |
| Impact | Low, Medium, High |
| Priority | Overall risk level |
| Response Strategy | Avoid, Mitigate, Transfer, Accept |
| Owner | Responsible individual |
| Status | Open, Monitoring, Closed |

The risk register should be reviewed regularly.

---

# 7. Risk Response Strategies

Different risks require different responses.

### Avoid

Modify the project to eliminate the risk entirely.

### Mitigate

Reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk.

### Transfer

Assign responsibility to another party where appropriate.

### Accept

Acknowledge the risk while monitoring its status.

The chosen strategy should be proportionate to the significance of the risk.

---

# 8. Risk Monitoring and Review

Risk reviews should occur throughout the project lifecycle.

Typical review points include:

- Architecture reviews.
- Engineering checkpoints.
- Sprint or milestone reviews.
- Release planning.
- Post-release evaluations.

Regular monitoring ensures that emerging risks are identified before they become significant issues.

# 12. Closing Reflection

Every successful engineering project manages uncertainty as carefully as it manages implementation.

By identifying, assessing, and addressing risks throughout the engineering lifecycle, TechAndMe projects become more resilient, predictable, and sustainable.

Managing risk is an essential component of professional engineering.
